# General / Family Medicine Clinic Patterns — Saudi Arabia

This skill provides domain knowledge for analyzing patient data in general-practice and family-medicine clinics across Saudi Arabia. Use these patterns to identify care gaps, predict upcoming needs, and generate timely outreach.

---

## Chronic Disease Management

### Diabetes (السكري)
- **HbA1c testing**: every 3 months for uncontrolled patients, every 6 months for stable patients.
- **Annual screening bundle**: eye exam (فحص العيون), foot exam, kidney function (eGFR + urine albumin), lipid panel.
- **Fasting complications**: Ramadan is high-risk for diabetic patients. Flag any diabetic patient 2 weeks before Ramadan for a "Ramadan readiness" consultation to adjust medication and meal timing.
- **Engagement signal**: a diabetic patient who misses two consecutive HbA1c checks (6+ months gap) is at high risk of complications. Prioritize recall.

### Hypertension (ارتفاع ضغط الدم)
- **Visit cadence**: quarterly for uncontrolled, every 6 months for controlled.
- **Medication adherence**: common drop-off after 6 months. If a hypertension patient has no visit in 4+ months, trigger a gentle check-in.
- **Combo patients**: diabetes + hypertension is extremely common (~60% overlap in Saudi adults). Always check for both conditions when scoring recall urgency.

### Asthma (الربو)
- **Visit cadence**: every 3 months if poorly controlled, every 6 months if well-controlled.
- **Seasonal triggers**: dust storms (spring), cold air (winter), and heavy AC use (summer) can worsen symptoms.
- **Inhaler refill**: if no inhaler refill in 3+ months for a known asthmatic, they may have dropped off treatment.

## Preventive Care & Annual Checkups

- **Recommended**: yearly comprehensive checkup for adults 30+.
- **Saudi compliance**: only ~20-25% of eligible adults complete annual checkups. This represents a massive recall opportunity for clinics.
- **Typical checkup bundle**: CBC, lipid panel, fasting glucose, liver function, kidney function, thyroid (for women 35+), vitamin D (extremely common deficiency in Saudi Arabia).
- **Vitamin D**: over 80% of Saudi adults are deficient. If a clinic offers vitamin D testing, nearly every patient is a candidate.

## Vaccination (التطعيمات)

- **Flu vaccine (لقاح الإنفلونزا)**: annual, best administered Sep-Nov before flu season peaks (Nov-Feb). Target chronic disease patients, elderly 60+, and pregnant women first.
- **Childhood vaccinations**: follow MOH (Ministry of Health) schedule. Family clinics should track children's upcoming doses and remind parents.
- **Travel vaccines**: Hajj/Umrah pilgrims may need meningococcal vaccine (required) and seasonal flu vaccine (recommended). Peak demand Jul-Nov depending on Hajj timing.
- **COVID boosters**: follow current MOH guidance, typically annual for high-risk groups.

## Seasonal Illness Patterns

| Season | Months | Common Presentations |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Winter | Nov-Feb | Flu, upper respiratory infections, asthma flares |
| Spring | Mar-May | Allergies, dust storm-related respiratory issues |
| Summer | Jun-Aug | Dehydration, heat stroke, UTIs, food poisoning, travel-related illness |
| Ramadan | Varies | Diabetic hypoglycemia, dehydration, headaches, gastric issues from iftar overeating |

## Clinic-Specific Behavioral Patterns

### Walk-in vs Appointment Culture
- Many small Saudi clinics are walk-in heavy (60-70% of visits). AI should not penalize patients for irregular scheduling — look at visit frequency, not appointment adherence.
- Shift to appointment-based care is a growth opportunity. Offer "priority booking" as a value-add over walk-in waits.

### Family Booking Patterns
- One family member (usually the mother) often books for the entire household: spouse, children, elderly parents.
- When analyzing a patient, check if other patients share the same phone number or address — they are likely family. A family recall campaign is more effective than individual outreach.

### Insurance-Driven Visits
- Many patients rush to use insurance benefits before their policy year-end (often Dec 31 or varies by employer).
- October-December sees a spike in "use it or lose it" checkups and elective procedures.
- Campaign opportunity: "Your insurance benefits expire soon — book your annual checkup" in Q4.

## Re-engagement Risk Scoring

| Missed Appointments | Risk Level | Recommended Action |
|---------------------|------------|--------------------|
| 1 missed | Low | Friendly reminder, reschedule offer |
| 2 missed | Medium | Personal call or WhatsApp from clinic |
| 3+ missed or 6+ month gap | High | Re-engagement campaign with incentive |
| 12+ month gap | Lapsed | "We miss you" campaign, possible free consultation offer |

For chronic disease patients, halve these thresholds — a diabetic missing one appointment is already medium risk.
