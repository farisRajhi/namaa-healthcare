## Saudi Patient Behavior Patterns

### Compliance Rates
- **Botox**: ~50% return on schedule. 30% return late (7-9mo). 20% lapse
- **Fillers**: ~40% return within 12 months. Many wait until fully dissolved
- **Laser hair removal**: ~60% complete full course (6+ sessions). Major drop-off after session 3-4
- **HydraFacial**: ~35% maintain monthly. Most do 2-4 then stop until event
- **Chemical peels**: ~45% complete recommended course. Better when sold as package
- **PRP**: ~50% complete initial 3 sessions. Only ~25% continue to maintenance phase
- **Acne treatment**: ~55% stay through 3 months. Better compliance in teens (parental involvement)

### Cultural Factors
- Cosmetic treatments are widely accepted and openly discussed among women
- Privacy is critical -- patients do not want to be seen in waiting areas by acquaintances
- Strong preference for female practitioners among female patients
- Social media heavily influences treatment decisions (Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok)
- Word-of-mouth referrals are the #1 driver. Happy patients refer 2-3 others
- "Natural look" is the most requested aesthetic outcome
- Skin lightening/brightening services have consistent year-round demand

## Re-engagement Windows

| Window | Days Overdue | Strategy | Expected Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prime | 0-30 | Simple reminder, mention visible results fading | 20-30% |
| Moderate | 31-90 | Before/after reminder, "maintain your results" | 12-20% |
| Late | 91-180 | New treatment options, package deal | 6-12% |
| Very lapsed | 180+ | New technology/service announcement, seasonal offer | 3-7% |

For active treatment courses (laser, PRP, microneedling): treat any gap >2x the scheduled interval as urgent. Results degrade and sessions may need to restart.

## Cross-sell Paths

Dermatology cross-sells work best when framed as complementary results, not upselling.

- Botox --> Fillers (natural pairing, "complete rejuvenation")
- HydraFacial --> Chemical peel ("deeper treatment for even better results")
- Chemical peel --> PRP ("accelerate skin renewal")
- Laser hair removal --> Skin brightening (treat same areas)
- Microneedling --> PRP (commonly combined in same session)
- Acne treatment --> Chemical peel / microneedling (post-acne scarring)
- Fillers --> Skin tightening ("complementary lift and volume")
- Any facial --> IV vitamin drip ("beauty from inside out")
- Botox returning patient --> New areas (forehead patient --> crow's feet, jaw slimming)

## Seasonal Demand Patterns

### Pre-Eid Al-Fitr (3-6 weeks before)
- HIGHEST demand period. Book 4-6 weeks ahead
- Top services: Botox, fillers, HydraFacial, laser, skin brightening
- "جهزي بشرتك للعيد" (Get your skin ready for Eid)
- Many patients want to look refreshed after Ramadan fasting

### Ramadan
- Appointments shift to evening hours (after iftar)
- Fasting patients avoid invasive procedures
- Good time for: consultations, gentle facials, product sales
- IV drips popular post-iftar

### Summer (June-September)
- Wedding season = peak cosmetic demand
- Brides book 3-6 months of treatments pre-wedding
- Laser hair removal courses start here for winter completion
- Sun damage consultations increase post-summer travel

### Winter (November-February)
- Best time for: chemical peels, laser resurfacing, IPL (less sun exposure)
- Market as "recovery season" for skin
- Patients more willing to have downtime procedures

### National Day (September 23)
- Minor cosmetic rush, similar to small Eid effect

## Pricing & Package Strategy

- Cosmetic dermatology is 80%+ out-of-pocket in Saudi Arabia
- Patients actively compare prices across clinics (Google, social media, Haraj)
- Package deals significantly improve compliance:
  - Laser hair removal: sell 6-session package (30-40% better completion vs pay-per-session)
  - PRP: sell 3+1 (3 sessions + 1 free maintenance)
  - HydraFacial: monthly subscription or 4-pack
  - Botox: loyalty pricing for returning patients
- Payment installment options (Tamara, Tabby) increase conversion 20-30%
- "Friend referral" discount is the highest-converting offer type

## Message Timing Rules

- **Botox**: Gentle reminder at 3.5 months ("Your results are at their best -- let's keep it that way"). Stronger at 5 months ("Effects starting to fade")
- **Laser hair removal during course**: Reminder at 5 weeks. If missed at 7 weeks, emphasize "gaps reduce effectiveness and may require additional sessions"
- **PRP during course**: Reminder at 3 weeks. Urgent if >6 weeks gap ("results from previous sessions diminish")
- **HydraFacial**: Light touch at 3.5 weeks. No pressure -- these patients come when they want
- **Fillers**: First mention at 7 months. Not urgent -- let patient decide timing
- **Post-procedure check-in**: 48 hours after any invasive treatment (shows care, catches complications)

## Communication Preferences

- WhatsApp preferred (90%+ open rate in this demographic)
- Before/after photos in messages dramatically increase re-booking (with consent)
- Avoid clinical language -- use "refreshed," "glowing," "natural" not "injections," "needles"
- Arabic default. Many younger patients (18-30) prefer English or mixed
- Best times: Sunday-Thursday 11am-1pm, 7pm-9pm. Saturday evening also good
- Never message during prayer times
- Instagram DM is secondary channel for younger demographic
- Privacy: never mention specific treatments in messages that others might see. Use "your upcoming appointment" not "your Botox appointment"
